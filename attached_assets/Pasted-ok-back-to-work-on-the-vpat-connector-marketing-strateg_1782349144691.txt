Can You Submit to Claude Directory First?

Does Your GitHub/Landing Page Have Everything Needed?
Here's the exact pre-submission checklist based on what Anthropic actually rejects:

Your server code — check these first:

All tools must include readOnlyHint or destructiveHint annotations — missing annotations cause about 30% of rejections. Verify every single tool in your server has this. One missing annotation = rejected. 
Patterson Dental

Your server must use Streamable HTTP transport — SSE was deprecated in the March 2025 MCP spec. Confirm this in your server config. 
Patterson Dental

Your landing page needs:

Remote MCP servers should provide a stable privacy policy URL in their public docs and submission — a missing or incomplete privacy policy results in immediate rejection. Your privacy policy needs to live at a real stable URL like accessibility.groundlogic.ai/privacy — not just in the README. 
Patterson Support

What Anthropic's reviewers need from you:

Developers must provide a standard testing account with sample data, and at least three working examples of prompts or use cases that demonstrate core functionality. 
Getkolla

Since your tool has no auth, the "testing account" requirement doesn't apply — but you need to provide three concrete example prompts like:

"Scan accessibility.groundlogic.ai and generate a VPAT report"
"Check fswdentalcare.replit.app for WCAG violations"
"Generate accessibility fixes for [URL]"
Beta Disclaimer — Yes, Add It

This protects you legally, sets honest expectations, and actually builds trust rather than undermining it. Compliance-aware developers respect honesty about tool limitations.

Google Form Feedback — Yes, Definitely
Simple and smart. Create a Google Form with just four fields:

What URL did you scan?
Did the tool work as expected? (Yes / Partially / No)
What broke or didn't work well? (text)
Feature requests? (text)
Embed the link in your README right under the beta disclaimer and on your landing page. Two reasons this matters beyond just collecting feedback: it signals to Claude directory reviewers that you're actively maintaining the tool, and it gives you real user data to point to on your college applications — "received feedback from X developers across Y institutions."
